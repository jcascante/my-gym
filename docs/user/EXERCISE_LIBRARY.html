💪 Exercise Library

The catalog of exercises your future workout programs will be built from

Overview Foundation for Program Generation

Behind the scenes, MyGym now has a library of exercises — each one tagged with the muscles it works, the equipment it needs, how difficult it is, and any joint or injury concerns it might not be great for. This is the raw material the program-generation feature (coming soon) will draw from when it builds your personalized workouts.

✓ There's nothing for you to set up here. The library is built in and maintained by MyGym — you won't add or edit exercises yourself. This page explains what's in it and how it's organized, so the logic behind your future programs isn't a black box.

What's In the Library

Every exercise includes:

  • Name and instructions — clear, step-by-step how-to
  • Form cues — the 2-3 things to focus on to do it safely and effectively
  • Muscles worked — a primary target plus any secondary muscles involved
  • Equipment needed — matched to the same equipment list you pick from in Training Environments, so a program can be built around what you actually have
  • Difficulty level — beginner, intermediate, or advanced
  • Contraindications — joints or areas (like knee, shoulder, or lower back) that this particular exercise loads, so it can be avoided or swapped if you've noted a limitation in your profile

How Exercises Are Grouped

Rather than just sorting exercises by body part, MyGym groups them the way a trainer or physical therapist actually thinks about them: by the type of movement first, and by muscle second.

Movement Pattern

Every exercise belongs to one fundamental movement pattern — things like squat, hinge (think deadlifts), push, pull, carry, or rotation. Two exercises in the same movement pattern train your body in a similar way, even if they use completely different equipment.

💡 Why this matters to you: if you have a knee issue and a program calls for a squat-pattern exercise, this classification is what will eventually let MyGym swap in a hinge-pattern exercise instead (like a Romanian deadlift) — one that works similar muscles without loading your knee the same way. Sorting exercises by muscle alone can't make that distinction; sorting by movement pattern can.

Equipment Variants

Most movements come in several equipment versions — a bench press, for example, exists as a barbell, dumbbell, resistance-band, and machine variant. They all train the same movement, so if your gym doesn't have a bench press station, MyGym can point you to an equally effective alternative you can actually do.

Troubleshooting

❓ Can I add my own exercise?

Not yet — the library is currently curated and maintained by MyGym, not user-editable. That may change alongside a future custom-program feature.

❓ Will this replace my Training Environments equipment list?

No — they work together. Your Training Environments equipment list says what you have access to; the exercise library's equipment tags are what individual exercises need. The upcoming program generator will match the two.

❓ My profile lists an injury or limitation — will the library account for that?

Each exercise notes which joints or areas it loads, but connecting that to the free-text limitations you enter in your profile is part of the not-yet-built program-generation engine, not this library on its own.
