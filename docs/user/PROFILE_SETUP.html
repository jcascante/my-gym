👤 Profile Setup

Customize your fitness profile to get personalized workouts

Overview

Your MyGym profile stores your personal fitness information and preferences. This data helps us create workout programs tailored specifically to you. You can create or update your profile anytime, and you can modify any information as your fitness journey progresses.

Before You Start

  • You must be logged into your MyGym account
  • You should have basic information ready: height, weight, fitness level
  • Think about your fitness goals (optional, but helpful)
  • Consider any injuries or physical limitations

How to Create or Update Your Profile

  1. Click on "My Profile" from the main menu
  2. Click the "Edit Profile" button (if you already have a profile)
  3. Fill in or update your information:
    • Age - Your current age (13+)
    • Gender - Male, Female, or Other
    • Height & Weight - Your measurements (we support decimal values)
    • Fitness Level - Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced
    • Activity Level - How active you are day-to-day
    • Workout Preferences - Days per week, duration, equipment access
    • Fitness Focus - What you want to achieve (strength, endurance, etc.)
    • Goals - Your short and medium-term fitness targets
    • Injuries or Limitations - Any physical constraints we should know about
  4. Click "Save Changes" to save your profile

Clearing Information

You can clear any profile field by leaving it empty or selecting "Select..." in dropdown menus. This is useful if you want to remove outdated information or change your preferences.

  1. Click "Edit Profile"
  2. Clear the field you want to remove (leave it empty)
  3. Click "Save Changes"

Understanding Your Profile Fields

Personal Information

Age, Gender, Height, Weight — These measurements help us understand your fitness context and adjust workout intensity appropriately. Height and weight support decimal values (e.g., 175.5 cm, 72.3 kg).

Fitness Level

Experience Level — Your history with structured exercise. Beginner means you're new to fitness; Advanced means you have years of consistent training.

Activity Level — How active you are outside formal workouts. "Sedentary" means mostly sitting; "Very Active" means you're on your feet most of the day.

Workout Preferences

Days per Week & Duration — How many days you want to work out and for how long. Realistic expectations help us create achievable programs.

Equipment Access — Home (minimal/bodyweight), Gym (full facility), or Bodyweight only. This shapes which exercises we recommend.

Fitness Focus — What you want to prioritize: Strength, Endurance, Flexibility, Weight Loss, Muscle Gain, or General Fitness.

Goals & Limitations

Short & Medium-term Goals — Specific targets you want to achieve. Examples: "Build a consistent workout habit" (short-term) or "Gain 5kg of muscle" (medium-term).

Injuries or Limitations — Any physical constraints we should know about: back pain, knee injuries, mobility issues, etc. This helps us avoid dangerous movements.

Troubleshooting

❓ Can I leave fields empty?

Yes! Most profile fields are optional. We'll create a program based on whatever information you provide. However, more details help us tailor your workouts better.

❓ My decimal measurements aren't saving (175.5 cm)

Decimal values are now fully supported. If you're having issues, try clearing the field and re-entering the value. Make sure you're using a period (.) not a comma (,) for decimals.

❓ Can I change my profile anytime?

Absolutely! You can update your profile as often as you want. Changes take effect immediately and apply to new workouts generated after the update.

❓ Will changing my profile affect existing workouts?

No. Changes only apply to new workouts. Workouts you've already started keep their original program. You can regenerate your program anytime to get a fresh workout plan based on your updated profile.

❓ How do I clear a field I no longer want?

Click "Edit Profile," clear the field (leave it empty or select "Select..." in dropdowns), then click "Save Changes." The field will be removed from your profile.

Tips & Tricks

  • 🎯 Be honest about your fitness level — Overestimating can lead to workout intensity that's too high. Start conservative; you can always increase difficulty later.
  • 📝 Document your goals — Specific, measurable goals help us design programs that actually get you there. "Get stronger" is harder to work with than "Deadlift 300 lbs."
  • ⚠️ Mention injuries upfront — Let us know about any pain points or limitations. We'll adapt workouts so you can train safely and avoid re-injury.
  • 🔄 Update regularly — As you progress, your fitness level changes. Update your profile every few months to keep workouts challenging and relevant.
  • 🏠 Set realistic expectations — Honestly assess how many days per week and how long you can realistically commit. A realistic plan beats an ambitious one you won't follow.
